I build products people keep open all day.

The software I care about isn’t used occasionally. It becomes part of how someone works. A notes app. A meeting tool. A dictation app. The best ones get out of the way, so you focus on the work instead of the tool.

Today I work at Voicenotes, an AI voice and meeting-notes app used by more than 800,000 people. Over the past year, I designed the foundation for the core flows of the new iOS app, built the native Mac app from scratch, and now design across the Mac app, web app, landing pages, and brand.

Voicenotes won a Product Hunt Golden Kitty Award, was featured among Apple’s top productivity apps, and appeared on Stripe’s Black Friday Cyber Monday website.

Before that, I worked on Buy Me a Coffee, redesigning memberships, payments, and growth flows, and improving the creator dashboard experience for a platform that processed 2.41 million payments and paid more than 50,000 creators in 2025.

Last year I shipped 31 projects across 9 apps and 4 platforms — iOS, macOS, Android, and web. I spend most of my time turning rough ideas into working products. I prototype in Figma and code, often with AI tools, then sweat the typography, motion, and detail until it feels obvious.
